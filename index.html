About

A concise narrative for recruiters, collaborators, and research teams.

My background is centered on designing research hardware under real constraints: tight form factors, custom actuation, fluidic and pneumatic components, sensor integration, and experimental validation. Rather than staying within a single product category, I have repeatedly built systems for new research needs and translated abstract ideas into working prototypes.

I am especially interested in the physical layer of autonomous experimentation: reliable hardware, modular mechanisms, and custom robotic tools that make advanced research workflows executable, repeatable, and scalable.
